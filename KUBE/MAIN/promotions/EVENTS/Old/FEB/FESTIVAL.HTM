Festival Sundiata
pronounced Soon-jah-tah
KUBE 93 is proud to support Festival Sundiata - the Pacific Northwest's African and African American festival. Now in its 17th year, Festival Sundiata celebrates the significant history and vast artistic contributions of Black people - past and present.

The Festival is named in honor of Sundiata, a legendary Mallan King who rescued his people's Jali or Griot (storyteller and keeper of the oral history), who had been kidnapped by a rival ruler as part of an attempt to usurp the empire.  Sundiata's acts of heroism restored the richness and strength of Mallan heritage to the people and began on of the most prosperous periods in the history of the empire.

When: Friday, Feb. 13th through Monday, Feb. 16th
Noon - 10:30 p.m. Friday and Sunday
Noon - 6 p.m. on Monday **

Where: Seattle Center - Seattle Center House, Flag Pavilion and
Conference Center.

Cost: Admission to the Festival is FREE - with the exception
of Friday night's pre-festival fundraiser.

** Monday is KUBE 93 Day is the Center House, featuring KUBE 93 personalities from 1:00 - 4:00 p.m. on the Center Stage.  Activities include performances by local artists such as The Tribal Music Group, Source of Labor, Akneel, For Tha Recordz, Ricochet and more, hosted by Tony B.

Festival Sundiata features a marketplace in the Flag Pavilion where merchants will sell ethnic foods and an array of hand crafted items, clothing, art and jewelry from Africa and the Northwest. There will also be a hip hop forum and showcase, a jazz and blues showcase, literary readings, a film festival, dance workshops, children's art workshops, visual artists, art gallery, and other activities throughout the weekend.
