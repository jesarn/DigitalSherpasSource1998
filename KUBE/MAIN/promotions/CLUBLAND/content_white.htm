YOU NEED A JAVA BROWSER TO SEE THE HOT PATROL TICKER...
For Mar. 7 to 14: Updated each Thursday

Gerald Collier brings his reflective lyrics and plaintive music to The Breakroom Feb. 21.









 By Kathy Mar, Sidewalk

Breaking news: This week you get two chances to catch the ex-Presidents: Chris Ballew (former Presidents of the USA singer) unveils his new project, The Giraffes, with a listening party at the Showbox. Then he'll be showing up at the Croc Cover Night on Saturday to belt out a few '70s classics with 70 Proof (featuring Ballew's former bandmate, drummer Jason Finn) ... In the Mardi Gras department, Seattle's own version of the Fat Tuesday celebration/inebriation runs for the next five days in Pioneer Square ... Hold your breath time: Pearl Jam has announced tour dates — the band'll be playing in Seattle July 21 at an as-yet-unannounced venue ... Space Monkeys moved their show from RKCNDY to the Croc; tickets will be honored or can be refunded.

Hot ticket: Croc Cover Night on Saturday sounds pretty promising.

LOCAL ACTION

Thursday, Feb. 19:

Super Deluxe welcomes Fat Tuesday revelers (and perhaps a few Real Worlders) to the Fenix.
Nashville Pussy cusses and such at the Croc.
The Giraffes (Chris Ballew's new project) host a CD release listening party at the Showbox.
Pat Metheny Group imbues the Opera House with eclectic sounds.
Ad Vielle Que Pourra plays traditional French music at the Tractor.

Friday, Feb. 20:

Gas Huffer electrifies the Croc.
Big Bad Voodoo Daddy brings big band back to the Fenix.
Sixteen Deluxe and Quasi play good ol' fashioned alternative music at The Breakroom.
Idiot Flesh, the quintessential Mardi Gras band, livens things up at Sit & Spin.
Tad and his massive sound saturate the OK Hotel.
NOFX = punk rock full stop at RKCNDY (SOLD OUT).
Rockabilly Hell continues with Paul Burlison at the Tractor.
Dread Zeppelin, quite possibly the only reggae-Elvis-and-Zeppelin cover band in existence, is at Doc Maynard's.
Hit Explosion funks up the Showbox.

Saturday, Feb. 21:

Croc Cover Night reanimates the '70s and brings you impostor versions of Devo and Guided by Voices.
Sky Cries Mary is the eye of the Fat Tuesday storm at the Fenix.
Gerald Collier brings his lonesome cowboy blues to The Breakroom.
New York in June swings by the Century Ballroom.
Willis knows what it's talking about at the Tractor.
Model Rockets harmonize and look good doing it at Sit & Spin.
Dub Narcotic Sound System headlines an all-ages show at the Velvet Elvis.
NOFX = punk rock full stop at RKCNDY (SOLD OUT).
Pegboy is at the OK Hotel.

Sunday, Feb. 22:

Lights Low — Volume High features music videos you'll probably never see on MTV, at the Speakeasy.
The Original Wailers bring reggae beats to the Fenix.
Don't Tell Mom! It's a Sunday night jazz romp with Be-bop & Destruction and the Marriot Jazz Quintet at the Showbox.
The OK House Band features all sorts of "special guests" — take your chances at the OK Hotel.
Studio 77 resurrects glam and punk music, with surprise live musicians dropping by at Linda's Tavern.
Connie Dover sings traditional Celtic music at the Tractor.

Monday, Feb. 23:

Space Monkeys bring their acid-jungle-trip-hop-rock all the way from Manchester to the Croc.
Be-bop & Destruction have arranged a little jazz for all you hepcats at the OK Hotel.

Tuesday, Feb. 24:

Max Roach, "The Duke Ellington of Drums," begins a six-night run at Dimitriou's Jazz Alley.
Michelene Impossible plays dreamy rock at the Croc.
Wild Rhododendrons jubilate in honor of Fat Tuesday at the Tractor.

Wednesday, Feb. 25:

Harvey Danger brings strange yet catchy rock to the Croc.
Pomade shakes it up with Ray Condo and the Ricochets at the Showbox.
Other Sounds features Key Ransone (electronica) and Jarrad Powell (gamelan) at the Speakeasy.
Monty Banks & The Highrollers swing at the Century Ballroom.

On the horizon
Marcy Playground and Matchbox 20, both Feb. 27; Days of the New Feb. 28; Holly Cole and Jeb Loy Nichols March 1; The Damned March 4; Megadeth March 6; The Crystal Method March 8; Hepcat March 9; Engine 54 March 13; Fatboy Slim March 14; Reel Big Fish March 18; Chumbawamba March 22, and Radiohead and Spiritualized April 5.
