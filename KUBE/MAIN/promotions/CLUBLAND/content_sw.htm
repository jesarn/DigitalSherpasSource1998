For the week of April 16 to 22: Updated each Thursday

Former Throwing Muse Kristin Hersh plays two solo acoustic shows at the OK Hotel.

   By Kathy Mar, Sidewalk

Breaking news: Friday it's "Culture Under Fire" night — clubs listed below with an asterisk (*) next to them will donate a portion of the night's proceeds to JAMPAC, the Joint Artists and Music Promotions Political Action Committee. ... Where are they now? Seattle! Morris Day & The Time, Jimmy Page and Robert Plant, Sebastian Bach, Quiet Riot, Gary Numan and Vanilla Ice are but a few of the old-school funk-metal-headbangin'-new wave-faux-rappers slated for visits in the near future. ... Meredith Brooks of "Bitch" fame will play an all-ages show on May 8. ... Save your pennies for the SAM Goes Black & White Ball, featuring Los Lobos. And finally, Zoot Suit Sunday makes its swingin' debut this week at the Showbox.

Hot ticket: Mark Eitzel Thursday and former Throwing Muses singer Kristin Hersh next Monday and Tuesday.

LOCAL ACTION

 

Thursday, April 16:

Mark Eitzel sings his heart and soul out at the Croc.
Damon & Naomi swoon prettily at ARO.space.
The Paperboys start rompin' at the Tractor.
June of 44 plays like a house on fire at The Breakroom.
Dodi headlines at the Showbox.
The Mr. T Experience pities fools at Sub Zero.
Shadowlight heralds the dawn of nocturne at the Fenix.
Guitar Monks strum and pluck at the Baltic Room.

 

Friday, April 17:

Green Apple Quickstep plays "Homegrown" music at the Showbox.*
Reverend Horton Heat motor-vates the crowd at the Paramount.
The Paperboys continue stompin' at the Tractor.
Chrome gets heavy at the Croc.*
Goodness rocks steady at Sub Zero.
Citizens' Utilities freshen things up at The Breakroom.
Anodyne provides relief at the OK Hotel.
New Sweet Breath headlines at Sit & Spin.*
H.B. Radke & The Jet City Swingers croon and swing at the Century Ballroom.
DJ Trent Von spins dance tunes at the Fenix Underground.*
Step lively at J.O.Y. F.M. at ARO.space.*

 

Saturday, April 18:

Lee Press-on and the Nails do that jazzy jump swing thing at the Croc.
Sleater-Kinney kicks booty for the kids at RKCNDY.
Semisonic sings "Closing Time" early at the Showbox, before Electrolush.
Greyboy Allstars create smooth beats at the Fenix.
Carmine alt-rocks at the OK Hotel.
Man Ray glamorizes The Breakroom.
Jazz is Dead plays, well, jazz renditions of Grateful Dead songs at the Ballard Firehouse.
Omar Torrez brings Tex-Mex to Sit & Spin.
Afrodisiacs funk up West Seattle's Rocksport.
Kerosene Dream rises from the ashes at the Tractor.
Snackbar features frenzied dancing and a cabaret at ARO.space.
The Foundation and The Con Men spin mad cuts at the Art Bar.

 

Sunday, April 19:

Kronos Quartet plays a homecoming show at the Opera House.
Redman gets ill at the Fenix.
Karen Schwartz and Evan Brubraker sing at the Tractor.
The OK House Band is back at the OK Hotel.
Yerba Buena plays Cuban music at the Century Ballroom.
Zoot Suit Sunday Lindy Hops into the Showbox.
Get down with An Evening With Dr. Strangelove at ARO.space.

 

Monday, April 20:

Kristin Hersh quietly electrifies at the OK Hotel.
Suitcase plays the Mind & Matter Adventure Club's anniversary party at the Croc.
Incubus invades RKCNDY.
DJ Leslie$ maintain$ decorum at Club Hi-Dee 'Ho at the Fenix.

 

Tuesday, April 21:

Kristin Hersh subtly hypnotizes at the OK Hotel.
Bill Frisell jazzes up the Tractor.
Richard Davies and his lush pop invade the Croc.
Parc Boys celebrate the release of their new CD at ARO.space.
Bluesman Joe Louis Walker begins a six-night run at Jazz Alley.
The Dave Nelson Band jams at the Fenix.
Wayne Horvitz and Michael Bisio do a low-key jazzy thang at the Baltic Room.

 

Wednesday, April 22:

Bill Frisell returns for another gig at the Tractor.
Easy Big Fella brings ska stylings to the Croc.
Cherry Bomb ignites ARO.space.
DJ Hubba Hubba presides at Pomade at the Showbox.
Monty Banks swings at the Century Ballroom.


On the horizon
Quasi, Hi Fi Killers and Sandy Silva April 23; Modest Mouse April 23-24; Bad Livers, God Lives Underwater and Morris Day & The Time April 24; Robin Holcomb April 25; Fastball April 26; The Chieftains and Dragonfly April 30; Gas Huffer and John Wesley Harding May 1; Tori Amos May 3; Meredith Brooks May 8; Bad Religion May 9; Quiet Riot May 14; Dave Matthews Band and Gary Numan May 15; Bob Dylan May 16-17; Alana Davis May 18; Ray Davies May 20; Sonic Youth May 22-23; Vanilla Ice May 24; Sebastian Bach May 31; Phish July 16-17; Backstreet Boys Aug. 14.
