(NEW YORK) - Plans are under way for a reunion of Nate Dogg, Snoop Doggy Dogg and Warren G, all Long Beach, Calif., natives who rapped together locally under the name 213 before signing record deals.

Each is working on a solo album on which the others will guest; the first reunion track is slated for Nate Dogg's upcoming album, due in late spring on his Dogg Foundation label. Warren G says that after the solo albums are released, the trio will record an album together as 213, with production assistance from Dr. Dre. A label for the set has not been determined; Snoop Doggy Dogg is signed to Death Row, and Warren G is on Def Jam. The trio also plans to tour together.

(NEW YORK) - The Guinness Fleadh, the two-day festival of Irish music held last June in New York, is expanding this summer.

In addition to returning to New York's Randall's Island on June 13 and 14, festivals will be held in Chicago on June 20 and San Francisco on June 27. Artists and venues have yet to be announced.

Last year's co-producers, Festival Marketing and the Mean Fiddler Organisation, have entered into a multi-year agreement with the Guinness Import Co. to sponsor the event.
