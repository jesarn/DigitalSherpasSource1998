(NASHVILLE) - The First Annual World Rap Music Awards will be held in Puerto Rico at the Colisco Ruben Rodgriquez Arena Feb. 27, with Ice-T and Prince Whipper Whip as hosts.

Invited to participate are Portrait, Terry Robinson, Teena Marie, Lighter Shade of Brown, Eric Dickerson, Barrio Boyz, Hiroko, DJ Quick, Full Force, El Debarge, Yo-Yo, Basheerah A. Ahmad and others.

The Henneyway Entertainment Corp. in association with Famous Group Entertainment is producing FAWRMA.

The winners have been chosen for the 1997 Online Music Awards, and while some artists like Spice Girls, Bush and Will Smith celebrate the accolades, others like Marilyn Manson, Mariah Carey and Kiss may end up laughing their way up to the podium.

Since Feb. 1, more than 100,000 cyber-critics logged on to AOL to help determine the winners of this year's Online Music Awards. Viewers were given the chance to cast their votes in 20 categories, some of which proved to be downright silly.

The Spice Girls walked away with an "Album of the Year" award, while Baby Spice was adorned as the cyber-world's "Favorite Spice Girl." Voters took the liberty of awarding rock's Bad Boy, Marilyn Manson, with "Best Album for Torturing Your Parents" and "Artist Most Likely to be Damned to Hell."

Chumbawamba will be remembered as "Favorite One-Hit Wonder" while Fresh Prince Will Smith took home "Favorite Guilty Pleasure" and "Best Male Singer." Jewel beat Mariah Carey for "Best Female Singer," while Carey instead was honored as "Artist Most in Need of a Good Spanking." Bush's Gavin Rossdale was tapped as "Pin-Up Artist of 1997," and his group was named "The Band Who Most Rocked Your World."

Perhaps the most interesting results of this year's poll gave props to Notorious B.I.G. for "Flyest Hip-hop Artist Ever ... in 1997," Chemical Brothers for "Best Knob Twiddlers," KISS for "Reunions We Never Thought We'd See In a Million Years (And Kind of Wish We Hadn't)" and comedian David Spade for "Dreamiest Hanson Brother." (Lana Fanelli)

Taking their cue from the famous YIPPIE prank (attempting to levitate the Pentagon in 1968), a loose coalition of artists, entertainers, label and film company employees calling themselves the Music Militia has announced that they will levitate Radio City Music Hall on Wednesday night in protest of "mediocre corporate drivel" (presumably, The Grammys).

According to a press release issued by the elusive Transcendent Recordings, thousands of Music Militia members will surround the venue at 6 p.m. engaging in an aural assault with horns, drums, voices and "noisemakers of all descriptions." The resulting ruckus will "liberate music from corporate accountants" and levitate Radio City Music Hall.

After their unparalleled telekinetic feat, the Music Militia will reconvene at New York's Coney Island High for the Anti-Grammy Awards. The following Anti-Grammy winners have already been announced and, according to a venue spokesperson, will be performing at the event: Best Heavy Performance: Candiria; Best Rant/Spoken Word: Rev. Ivan Stang; Best New Artist: Osiva; Lifetime Achievement Award: John Hall (King Missile); Best Acoustic Performance: Grass. (Isaac Josephson)
