On Sunday, March 1, Madonna will grant her fans an all-access pass into the making of her new album, Ray of Light.

For the first time since the beginning of her career, Madonna will allow cameras inside the recording studio as MTV News anchor Kurt Loder goes one-on-one in an exclusive interview with the Material Girl.

"Inside Madonna" will take viewers on a tour through the recording studio and on the set of her new video for "Frozen," the first single released off of Ray of Light. During the interview, Madonna talks openly about her transition into motherhood, her recent interest in "Kabbalah," a Jewish form of mysticism, and the inspiration behind the new album.

The interview also captures a cameo appearance by Madonna's daughter, Lourdes, marking her first-ever television appearance. (Lana Fanelli)

The Titanic soundtrack is still barreling ahead with all engines full steam, but the top of the nation's music sales charts have some new blood, courtesy of southern gangsta rap and an '80s-based flick.

Titanic's still No. 1 by a long shot, selling 562,000 copies for the week ending February 22, according to SoundScan. (That makes four million copies sold in just 11 weeks for the soundtrack; during all of 1997 just two records, Jewel's Pieces of You and the Spice Girls' Spice, sold more copies.)

But making a run at the top spot is rapper Master P's younger brother, New Orleans' own Silkk the Shocker. His Charge It 2 Da Game debuts at No. 3, selling 248,000 copies, while the soundtrack to Adam Sandler's The Wedding Singer, featuring the flashback sounds of Culture Club, the Smiths, New Order and Musical Youth (Class of '84, anyone?), bounds from No. 26 all the way to No. 9, selling 89,000 copies.

The other noteworthy debut was singer/songwriter Ani DiFranco's Little Plastic Castle. Released on her own Righteous Babe Records, the record comes in at a lofty No. 22. For one week, at least, the indie babe from Buffalo, N.Y., managed to outsell mainstream pop diva Mariah Carey, whose Butterfly logged in at No. 24.

From the top it was Titanic, followed by Celine Dion's Let's Talk About Love (selling 288,000 copies); Charge It 2 Da Game; Savage Garden (105,000); Pearl Jam's Yield (102,000); Backstreet Boys (99,000); Usher's My Way (95,500); Spice Girls' Spice World (95,000); The Wedding Singer; and Matchbox 20's Yourself or Someone Like You (87,000). (Eric Boehlert for Rolling Stone Network)

(LONDON) - London Records' double Brit-winning female quartet All Saints are due to appear at Germany's Echo Awards on March 5 in Hamburg, despite not being nominated in any category.

Best national artist nominee Peter Maffay is on the bill, as are hip-hop star Sabrina Setlur and Aqua. Rappers Jazzy and Lee of trio Tic Tac Toe will perform under the moniker Tic Tac Two.

The show will be broadcast in edited form the following day on Germany's ARD TV network. All Saints' single "Never Ever," currently No. 29 and climbing on the German charts, became a hit after the close of nominations.
