(NEW YORK) - The Fugees have recorded and lensed a video for "Happy To Be Me," a new song that will premiere on the "Sesame Street" special "Elmopalooza," scheduled to air Feb. 20 on ABC.

The clip, shot in New York City's Abingdon Square, features band members Lauryn Hill, Pras and Wyclef Jean frolicking with Big Bird and Snuffleupagus.

(LOS ANGELES) - On the heels of two strong weekends for the movie Spice World, Showtime Event Television has scheduled a second run of showings for the pay-per-view special "Spice Girls in Concert -- Wild" to run through Feb. 15.

Meantime, the "Bee Gees: One Night Only" concert taped in Las Vegas last November for a pay-per-view showing, will be aired on HBO at 10 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 14.

Chumbawamba has officially entered into a heated competition with Blur and Echo and the Bunnymen.

Last week it was announced that Blur and Echo would battle it out to record the song for this summer's England World Cup. Now Chumbawamba has offered a song to the English Football Association, according to the BBC. The platinum-selling group said they will record a special song to follow-up their two other singles, "Tubthumping" and "Amnesia." (Ari Bendersky)

Sean "Puffy" Combs will work his magic when he remixes a Jackson 5 hit for Motown 40 Forever, a Motown anniversary album, slated for release on Feb. 17.

Combs, aka "Puff Daddy," will pitch in for the celebration of Motown's 40th anniversary by presenting a remix of the Jackson 5's debut single, "I Want You Back." The song became one of four Jackson 5 singles to reach the No. 1 spot on the Billboard charts in 1970, making them Billboard's No. 1 artist of the year.

The two-CD set will cull 39 Motown classics that helped make the label a household name. Remixes of popular soul-stirring songs including the Four Tops' "I Can't Help Myself (Sugar Pie Honey Bunch),"Smokey Robinson's "Tears of a Clown," Diana Ross and the Supremes' "Someday We'll be Together" and The Commodores' "Three Times a Lady." (Lana Fanelli)

(NEW YORK) - The multi-CD album Crystal Ball, by the Artist Formerly Known as Prince, will also be available at Blockbuster and the Musicland Group, in addition to Best Buy, according to a source in the artist's camp.

Best Buy had said that it would be the sole distributor for the album, originally sold via the artist's Web site, but cautioned that the deal would not be an exclusive since the company had agreed to make the album available to merchants in markets where it doesn't have stores. However, nothing was finalized with Best Buy, say sources.
