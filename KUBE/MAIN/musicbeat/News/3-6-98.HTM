When the new Dave Matthews Band album hits stores, fans of the band will hear a familiar female voice: Alanis Morissette.

The album, Before These Crowded Streets, is set for an April 28 release on RCA Records and will feature Morissette singing on a few songs, according to a source for Dave Matthews. Banjo slayer Bela Fleck also shows up on numerous tracks.

The album was produced by Steve Lillywhite and was recorded in Sausalito, Calif., and New York. Our source described the sound as a definite progression from their last album, Crash, complete with complex layering.

The band completed the album last week and is getting set to tour. They will play an afternoon benefit show on April 18 at Victory Stadium in Roanoke, Va. Tickets for the event go on sale March 21 and cost $28.50. Proceeds will benefit Virginia-based charities.

Following the benefit concert, DMB will head down to play the New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival on April 26 and then head off on a Canadian tour. That tour kicks off May 2 in Montreal. DMB will play seven dates in Canada and end the tour in Vancouver on May 13. For a complete tour schedule, head over to the official Dave Matthews Band website. (Ari Bendersky)

Hootie & the Blowfish had to halt their South Carolina writing and rehearsal sessions so guitarist Mark Bryan could join his wife at the hospital after she went into labor.

The multiplatinum-selling pop artists were about to begin interviewing producers to start pre-production on their third album in an old warehouse in Columbia, S.C., according to a source close to the band. However, the foursome -- Bryan, Jim "Soni" Sonefeld, Darius Rucker and Dean Felber -- had to postpone the sessions when Bryan's wife went into labor. The couple welcomed their baby girl into the world earlier this week.

A spokeswoman for the band told JAMTV that Hootie will begin recording the follow-up to Fairweather Johnson in about a month, once Bryan and his wife have settled into parenthood.

In related news, Hootie & the Blowfish will headline the fourth annual Monday after the Masters Pro-Am show, which will benefit Junior Golf. The concert, which will feature Hootie and some "special friends," will be held on April 13 at the Columbia, S.C., Township Auditorium. When JAMTV asked a band spokeswoman who the friends would be, she responded, "It could be Dan Marino. It could be Toad the Wet Sprocket." In other words, the band won't know until they hit the stage.

Tickets run $20.50 and are currently available. (Ari Bendersky)

The Backstreet Boys will hit "Saturday Night Live" on March 14.

The five member R&B-pop group consisting of Nick Carter, Howie Dorough, Brian Littrell, AJ McLean and Kevin Richardson have been scoring big points since the release of their self-titled debut. The album has gone triple platinum -- boasting sales of more than 11 million copies -- due largely in part to the group's first triple platinum single, "Quit Playing Games (With My Heart)." It currently sits at the No. 6 spot on the Billboard Top 200 Album chart.

The Boys' new single, "Everybody (Backstreet's Back)" will hit radio stations on March 9. A North American tour beginning July 8 is scheduled to kick off in Charlotte, N.C. (Lana Fanelli)

(NASHVILLE) - LeAnn Rimes has completed recording her next Curb album, a double set of some 22 songs to be titled Sittin' On Top Of The World.

Rimes says that the album is "a lot of country, a little bit of pop" and that she expects it to be released in May, preceded by the single "Commitment." She also says that she will likely start work soon on an autobiography.
