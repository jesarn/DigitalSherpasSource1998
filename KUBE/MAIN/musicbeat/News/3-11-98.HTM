(NEW YORK) - It's a triumphant week for Aretha Franklin, who sails onto the Billboard Hot 100 with the highest debut of the week.

"A Rose Is Still A Rose" (Arista), new at No. 43, is the first Franklin single to chart since "Willing To Forgive" peaked at No. 26 in 1994. It's only the third Franklin chart entry in the '90s, but it does extend the superstar's chart span to 37 years and two weeks, dating back to the debut of her Columbia single "Won't Be Long" the week of Feb. 27, 1961.

Dick's Picks Vol. 10, the latest live offering from the Grateful Dead, is ready for the takin'.

The three-CD set features songs culled from two shows the Dead performed at San Francisco's Winterland on Dec. 29 and 30, 1977. The show from the first night is described as "one of those shows" among Deadheads -- a performance that changed people's lives. For years, bootleg tapes of that show circulated throughout the Dead community and now it is available in one collection.

Dick's Picks Vol. 10 is currently available for $18.50 through mail order only from Grateful Dead Merchandising by calling 1-800-CAL-DEAD. (Ari Bendersky)

(LOS ANGELES) - Roy Orbison's MasterTone album Live At The BBC, which Best Buy is promoting in ads as an exclusive, is for sale at another retail outlet.

Gary Arnold, VP of marketing for Best Buy, says the 284-store chain and MasterTone have made the title available to the 17-store, Allston, Mass.-based Newbury Comics chain in the hopes that the album will qualify for the Billboard 200.

Titles sold exclusively at one chain are not eligible under Billboard chart criteria.
