(LOS ANGELES) - Rap star Coolio has signed on to star in indie feature Midnight Mass for executive producer Roger Avary (Killing Zoe) and first-time director Kevin Fukunaga.

Coolio will play Blue Lou, a Jamaican gang lord in San Pedro, Calif. Also starring in Midnight Mass are Wil Wheaton ("Star Trek: The Next Generation") and Taylor Negron ("Friends," "Hope and Gloria").

Midnight Mass depicts a young man's quest for truth, even as lies and hypocrisy threaten to bring on Judgment Day. The screenwriter is Stevo Polyi, who worked at the same Video Archives store that employed Avary and Quentin Tarantino.

Coolio's film credits include Dear God, Phat Beach and Batman & Robin. Shooting on Midnight Mass is slated to begin in May in Portland, Ore.

(NEW YORK) - Michael Jackson is without a manager.

According to the artist's spokesman, Bob Jones, Jackson's yearlong contract with Tarak Ben Ammar has expired; Jones would not comment on whether the singer will renew the contract or seek new management.

The former manager is an associate of Saudi prince Alwaleed Bin Talal Bin Abdulaziz Alsaud, who has several joint business ventures with Jackson, including the artist's MJJ Music label.

Danbert Nobacon, lead singer of Chumbawamba, was arrested Monday night after dumping a bucket of ice water on British Deputy Prime Minister John Prescott at the Brit Awards.

The singer for the anarchist pop group who made it big in 1997 with their hit single "Tubthumping" was taken away by guards from the award ceremony at Dockland Arena in London. Prior to his arrest, Nobacon leapt upon the table where Prescott and his family were watching Fleetwood Mac perform and dumped the water over his head.

Prescott's office released a statement Tuesday morning calling Nobacon's actions "deplorable and unacceptable public behavior." The statement continued: "It now appears that it was a publicity stunt designed to draw attention to the group's act. [Prescott] thinks it's utterly contemptible that his wife and other womenfolk should have been subjected to such cowardly, terrifying behaviour simply because they were accompanying a public figure at an event designed to support the British music industry."

Although Nobacon was taken into custody by Scotland Yard, no charges were pressed in the matter and he was later released. (Ari Bendersky)

(NEW YORK) - As part of its ongoing Jimi Hendrix reissue program, MCA/Experience Hendrix has set an April 21 release date for The Jimi Hendrix Experience: BBC Sessions.

The two-CD set contains tracks that the Jimi Hendrix Experience recorded in 1967-69 for airing on BBC radio, including many previously unreleased performances. Among the previously unissued cuts are versions of "Hey Joe," "Foxy Lady," "Manic Depression," and "I Was Made To Love Her"/"Ain't Too Proud To Beg" with Stevie Wonder.
