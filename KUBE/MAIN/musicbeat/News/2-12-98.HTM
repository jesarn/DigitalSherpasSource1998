Madonna may be coming to a club near you.

That's right, the Material Girl is tossing around the idea of scheduling a few small club dates in the United States to promote her upcoming album, Ray Of Light, which is scheduled for a March 3 release. So far, the only "confirmed" date is an appearance at the Roxy in New York this Saturday. Although Madonna's spokeswoman at Warner Bros. could not confirm this show, a representative from the Roxy told JAMTV Wednesday that Madonna is in fact performing there Saturday night. Word has it that Madonna will sing three to five songs off her forthcoming album.

Madonna's official website at Warner Bros. reports that Madonna will schedule a few club dates starting Feb. 14 (read: the Roxy show), however other than that, no other information is available.

In related news, starting Feb. 17, Madonna heads across the Atlantic to promote Ray of Light in Europe. She will make stops in Germany, France, England and Italy to perform live on various variety shows in those countries.

Stay tuned to JAMTV for more information. (Ari Bendersky)

(LOS ANGELES) - Rap star Coolio has signed on to star in indie feature Midnight Mass for executive producer Roger Avary (Killing Zoe) and first-time director Kevin Fukunaga.

Coolio will play Blue Lou, a Jamaican gang lord in San Pedro, Calif. Also starring in Midnight Mass are Wil Wheaton ("Star Trek: The Next Generation") and Taylor Negron ("Friends," "Hope and Gloria").

Midnight Mass depicts a young man's quest for truth, even as lies and hypocrisy threaten to bring on Judgment Day. The screenwriter is Stevo Polyi, who worked at the same Video Archives store that employed Avary and Quentin Tarantino.

Coolio's film credits include Dear God, Phat Beach and Batman & Robin. Shooting on Midnight Mass is slated to begin in May in Portland, Ore.

(LONDON) - V2 Records has signed U.S.-born singer/songwriter N'Dea Davenport, former vocalist in British soul-funk combo Brand New Heavies, to a worldwide deal.

The pact is thought to be an integral part of V2's deal to distribute and market product from Delicious Vinyl, to which Davenport was previously signed. Davenport, who has worked with Madonna, Bootsy Collins, Al Jarreau and Emmylou Harris, will release a solo album through V2 in the spring. The set, with production by Davenport, Daniel Lanois and Dallas Austin, will include covers of Neil Young's "Old Man" and Bobby Blue Bland's "Save Your Love."

(NEW YORK) - Comedy Central's outrageous animated television program "South Park" has been on the air less than a year, but the break-away hit already has enough momentum to warrant an album.

According to sources, Rick Rubin is putting an album together for Columbia that is due in August. Although song selection is far from complete, expect the album to prominently feature Isaac Hayes, who is the voice of "South Park" character Chef.

(NEW YORK) - As part of its ongoing Jimi Hendrix reissue program, MCA/Experience Hendrix has set an April 21 release date for The Jimi Hendrix Experience: BBC Sessions.

The two-CD set contains tracks that the Jimi Hendrix Experience recorded in 1967-69 for airing on BBC radio, including many previously unreleased performances. Among the previously unissued cuts are versions of "Hey Joe," "Foxy Lady," "Manic Depression," and "I Was Made To Love Her"/"Ain't Too Proud To Beg" with Stevie Wonder.
