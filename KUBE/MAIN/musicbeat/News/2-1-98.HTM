(NEW YORK) - Puff Daddy tops the nominees for the 12th annual Soul Train Music Awards.

His album with the Family, No Way Out, is nominated for album of the year and best R&B/soul album by a group, band, or duo; the single "I'll Be Missing You" with Faith Evans and 112 receives three nominations, including best R&B/soul single by a group and best music video.

Erykah Badu and God's Property also score big, with four nods each. Badu is up for best R&B/soul or rap new artist and receives nominations for her album Baduizm and single "On & On." God's Property, also a contender for new artist, gets nods for its self-titled album and the single "Stomp."

Other multiple nominees include Mary J. Blige and the Notorious B.I.G., with three each, and Missy "Misdemeanor" Elliott, with two.

The awards will be presented Feb. 27 at the Shrine Auditorium in Los Angeles.

Singer Bobby Brown, who first got his start with New Edition back in the '80s, was convicted in a DUI case on Thursday in a Florida courtroom.

Brown, 30, was finally brought to trial following an incident in 1996 when he crashed his black Porsche into bushes and a street sign in Hollywood, Fla. When the judge read Brown's sentence, the singer reportedly broke down and started crying. His wife, Whitney Houston, was said to be crying as well.

After the crash, Brown's blood alcohol level was tested at 0.2 percent, more than twice Florida's legal limit, according to legal testimony. Drugs were also discovered in his blood. Further, Brown broke four ribs and a foot in the crash, testimony showed.

Brown was ordered to spend five days in jail and undergo drug and alcohol treatment. His attorney, Robert Buonauro, questioned why it took four months to charge Brown with drunk driving and, after posting a $15,000 bond to keep Brown out of jail, said he would appeal the case, according to the Associated Press. (Ari Bendersky)

Just days after we reported that Duran Duran had parted ways with Capitol Records, JAMTV has learned EMI-Capitol Properties will release a Duran Duran remix album.

Essential Duran Duran: Night Versions is tentatively slated for an April 7 release and delves into the band's back catalog, culling music from their lengthy career with Capitol. The collection will feature all of the band's 12-inch mixes up through the "Wild Boys." Nick Rhodes, the band's original keyboardist, is reportedly busy writing the liner notes.

Track listing for Night Versions:

  1. Planet Earth (night version)
  2. Girls On Film (night version)
  3. My Own Way (night version)
  4. Rio (12" dance version)
  5. Hungry Like The Wolf (night version)
  6. New Religion (night version)
  7. Hold Back The Rain (remix)
  8. Is There Something I Should Know (monster mix)
  9. Union of the Snake (monkey mix)
  10. The Reflex (dance mix)
  11. Wild Boys (wilder than wild boys mix)
