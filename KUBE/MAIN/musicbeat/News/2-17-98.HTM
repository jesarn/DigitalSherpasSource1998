If you thought the Oscar and Felix tandem was odd, then the combination of hip-hop aristocrat Coolio and aging country & western star Kenny Rogers will seem downright abnormal.

Nonetheless, the rapper and the roper have teamed to re-record Rogers' famed "The Gambler." According to the song's producer, Devon Davis, Rogers will sing the hook and lead on the song, while Coolio will handle most of the verse duties. Davis has already laid down the backing track in a Los Angeles studio, which he calls "slamming."

"It's better than 'Gangsta's Paradise,'" he adds, comparing it to Coolio's biggest hit. "It's off the hook, it's tight." Davis says it was primarily his idea to re-record the song. "I used to have a gambling problem," he admits.

The song will initially be released as a single any time between late March and June, according to Davis, and later be included on a film soundtrack, Coolio's next album or Davis' debut, due out in June. A music video for 'The Gambler '98', featuring Coolio and Rogers, is also in the works, and will be shot in Atlanta or Los Angeles.

In related news, Coolio is close to ending his relationship with Tommy Boy Records and signing a new deal with a major label, reportedly Maverick Records. (Blair R. Fischer for Rolling Stone Network)

Oscar-nominated director Gus Van Sant spent some time with the brothers Hanson this past week putting the finishing touches on a video for their new single, "Weird."

The video was filmed over a four-day period in New York. In addition to Van Sant, Hanson teamed up with producer Danny Wolf (Allen Ginsberg's Ballad of the Skeletons), production designer Tom Foden (Michael Jackson and Janet Jackson) and director of photography Harris Savides (The Game).

Hanson has been nominated for three Grammys including "Best New Artist," "Best Pop Performance by Duo or Group" and "Album of the Year" for their multiplatinum-selling album, Middle of Nowhere. They are also slated to appear on this year's Grammy show, set to air on Feb. 25.

"Weird," Hanson's fourth single, hit airwaves on Monday. (Lana Fanelli)

The Artist will hold the party at his Minneapolis-based recording studio, Paisley Park. A special online chat with the Purple One and his wife, Mayte, will take place on AOL starting at 11 p.m. (EST).
